Here is the realization theorem in the form that seems strongest and safest.

## Realization theorem on the exact quotient

Let (X) be the accessible exact marked object, with deterministic successor (F), theorem-side event (\epsilon_4), phase (\kappa), and canonical clock
[
\beta=-\kappa \pmod m.
]
Assume the phase-corner package on (X): (\kappa(Fx)=\kappa(x)+1), the current event is governed by the small scheduler, and the short corner is the unique state detected by the two-step event signature
[
(\epsilon_4(x),\epsilon_4(Fx))=(\mathrm{flat},\mathrm{other}_{0010}).
]
That is exactly the shape already isolated in the theorem cleanup and the 070 corner-time note. ([d5_069_theorem_cleanup.md](./d5_069_theorem_cleanup.md), lines 221–267 and 374–399; [d5_corner_time_descent_theorem_20260314_070.md](./d5_corner_time_descent_theorem_20260314_070.md), lines 14–19)

Now let
[
\pi:X\to Q
]
be a deterministic quotient with induced successor (G) on (Q), and assume **current-event exactness**
[
\epsilon_4=\bar\epsilon_4\circ \pi
]
for some (\bar\epsilon_4:Q\to E). This is the key exactness hypothesis. No extra “two-step exactness” or future-word exactness is needed. ([d5_corner_time_descent_theorem_20260314_070.md](./d5_corner_time_descent_theorem_20260314_070.md), lines 21–27 and 50–63)

Then:

### 1. The short corner is visible on the quotient

Because the quotient is deterministic,
[
\pi(F^j x)=G^j\pi(x),
]
so
[
\epsilon_4(F^j x)=\bar\epsilon_4(G^j\pi(x)).
]
Hence the entire future (\epsilon_4)-word is already a function of the quotient state. In particular the short-corner detector descends:
[
C_Q(q)=1
\iff
(\bar\epsilon_4(q),\bar\epsilon_4(Gq))=(\mathrm{flat},\mathrm{other}_{0010}).
]
So the quotient sees the short corner intrinsically through the two-step event marker. On a single accessible (m)-cycle this picks out a unique corner state; on a union of components it defines the corner locus componentwise. ([d5_corner_time_descent_theorem_20260314_070.md](./d5_corner_time_descent_theorem_20260314_070.md), lines 28–63)

### 2. Corner-time descends

Assume each accessible component has exactly one future short corner in every (m)-step window. That is automatic on the one-corner (m)-cycle; on the chain-first version it holds on each full regular block, or after the natural cyclic completion. Define
[
T_Q(q):=\min{,r\in{0,\dots,m-1}:C_Q(G^r q)=1,}.
]
Then (T_Q) is well-defined and
[
T_Q(Gq)=T_Q(q)-1 \pmod m.
]
This is the descended corner-time clock. ([d5_corner_time_descent_theorem_20260314_070.md](./d5_corner_time_descent_theorem_20260314_070.md), lines 65–90)

### 3. The canonical clock is recovered from corner-time

On the theorem-side object, the short corner sits at phase (\kappa=2). Therefore the time to the next short corner is
[
T_X(x)=2-\kappa(x)\pmod m.
]
Since (\beta=-\kappa),
[
T_X(x)=\beta(x)+2 \pmod m,
\qquad\text{so}\qquad
\beta(x)=T_X(x)-2 \pmod m.
]
Because (T_X) depends only on the descended (\epsilon_4)-word, it factors through (Q), and the quotient clock
[
\beta_Q(q):=T_Q(q)-2 \pmod m
]
satisfies
[
\beta_Q\circ \pi=\beta,
\qquad
\beta_Q(Gq)=\beta_Q(q)-1 \pmod m.
]
So the canonical clock descends uniquely, and it is exactly corner-time with the fixed (-2) normalization. ([d5_corner_time_descent_theorem_20260314_070.md](./d5_corner_time_descent_theorem_20260314_070.md), lines 92–125)

## What the sufficient hypothesis really is

For the **corner-time route**, the sufficient exactness hypothesis is:

1. (Q) is a **deterministic** quotient of the accessible marked object;
2. the **current** event (\epsilon_4) factors through (Q);
3. each accessible component has one future short corner per period/block.

Only (1) and (2) are exactness hypotheses. Item (3) is the geometric/periodic input needed to define corner-time globally. So the precise answer is:

> **Current-event exactness for (\epsilon_4) is already enough.**
> You do not need separate two-step exactness, future-word exactness, or prior exactness of (\beta). ([d5_corner_time_descent_theorem_20260314_070.md](./d5_corner_time_descent_theorem_20260314_070.md), lines 24–63 and 116–130)

## The actual D5 caveat

This does **not** mean every plausible global (m)-state quotient is good enough. The 071 working note shows that the bare (\beta)-position quotient can still merge

* (\mathrm{flat}) with (\mathrm{other}_{1000}) at (\beta=m-2), and
* (\mathrm{flat}) with (\mathrm{other}_{0010}) at (\beta=m-3).

So “right drift” alone is insufficient; that quotient is not exact enough for current (\epsilon_4), and therefore it is not enough for corner-time descent. The obstruction is exactly the early post-carry decoration controlling the short corner. ([d5_marked_chain_rule_and_quotient_20260314_071.md](./d5_marked_chain_rule_and_quotient_20260314_071.md), lines 61–86)

Likewise, on each fixed marked chain the intended class already sees the full affine chain coordinate (s), but on the union of chains that coordinate is not yet a global deterministic quotient because of the hidden offset
[
\sigma=source_u+w.
]
So the live issue is still the global bridge: normalize away that offset, and make (\epsilon_4) exact on the glued quotient. ([d5_marked_chain_rule_and_local_quotient_071.md](./d5_marked_chain_rule_and_local_quotient_071.md), lines 65–130; [d5_071_unified_bridge_handoff.md](./d5_071_unified_bridge_handoff.md), lines 73–145)
