## Executive answer

The correct global refined bridge is **not** the static chain label `(beta,a,b)`.

The right splice-compatible exact bridge is a **dynamic boundary odometer**
\[
(\beta,\delta),
\qquad
\delta \in \mathbb Z / m^2 \mathbb Z,
\]
where `delta` increments by `+1` exactly at the wrap/splice and is fixed inside
a chain.

Equivalently, write the base-`m` digits of `delta` as
\[
\delta = q_0 + m \sigma,
\qquad
q_0,\sigma \in \{0,\dots,m-1\}.
\]
Then:

- `q_0` is the carry-jump chain label (the `q` field on the carry-jump row in
  the frozen data; I call it `q_0` here to avoid collision with the older
  per-chain running `q` gauge),
- `sigma = source_u + w (mod m)` is the normalized slice offset,
- `(a,b)` is a **readout** of `(q_0,\sigma)`, not the true splice state.

So the minimal theorem-shape answer is:

> the smallest splice-compatible exact bridge is the quotient with state
> `(beta,delta)`, where `delta` is the base-`m` boundary odometer and
> `delta -> delta+1` at each splice; current `epsilon4` factors exactly through
> the base-`m` digits of `delta`.

On the full abstract odometer this bridge has `m^3` states.
On a smaller splice-invariant accessible subset `A \subseteq Z/m^2`, it has
`m |A|` states.

## 1. The proposed exact bridge

### 1.1 Boundary state

Use the two base-`m` digits
\[
\delta \equiv q_0 + m \sigma \pmod{m^2},
\qquad
q_0,\sigma \in \{0,\dots,m-1\}.
\]

It is often clearer to write the same state as the pair
\[
\delta \leftrightarrow (q_0,\sigma).
\]

The static decoration bits from `072/073` are then
\[
a(\delta) = 1_{\{q_0=m-1\}},
\]
\[
b(\delta)
= 1_{\{q_0+\sigma \equiv 1 \!\!\!\pmod m\}}
  \;\vee\;
  \bigl(1_{\{q_0=m-1\}} \wedge 1_{\{\sigma\neq 1\}}\bigr).
\]

So `(a,b)` is a quotient of the boundary state.

### 1.2 Global bridge state

Let `A` be the accessible splice-invariant set of boundary states
`delta` on the regular chain union.
Define
\[
Q_m^{\mathrm{dyn}} := \{(\beta,\delta): \beta \in \{0,\dots,m-1\},\ \delta\in A\}.
\]

Here `beta` is the already-settled coarse carry/distance coordinate:
`beta = m-1` at carry-jump, `beta = 0` at wrap.

### 1.3 Successor law

The successor is

\[
F(\beta,\delta)=
\begin{cases}
(\beta-1,\delta), & \beta>0,\\[1mm]
(m-1,\delta+1), & \beta=0,
\end{cases}
\]
where `delta+1` is addition in `Z/m^2`.

In digit form this is exactly
\[
(q_0,\sigma)\mapsto
\begin{cases}
(q_0+1,\sigma), & q_0<m-1,\\
(0,\sigma+1), & q_0=m-1.
\end{cases}
\]

So the wrap/splice law is the canonical two-digit base-`m` odometer.

### 1.4 Current-event readout

Current `epsilon4` is read from `(\beta,\delta)` by

\[
\bar\epsilon_4(\beta,\delta)=
\begin{cases}
\mathrm{carry\_jump}, & \beta=m-1,\\
\mathrm{wrap}, & \beta=0,\\
\mathrm{other}_{1000}, & \beta=m-2 \text{ and } a(\delta)=1,\\
\mathrm{other}_{0010}, & \beta=m-3 \text{ and } b(\delta)=1,\\
\mathrm{flat}, & \text{otherwise.}
\end{cases}
\]

This recovers the `073` decorated rule, but now with a deterministic splice law.

## 2. Existence theorem

### Theorem 2.1 (splice-compatible decorated exact bridge)

Assume the accessible regular chain union admits coordinates
\[
x \longmapsto (\beta(x), q_0(x), \sigma(x))
\]
such that:

1. `beta` is the coarse bridge coordinate,
   with `beta -> beta-1` inside a chain and `0 -> m-1` across the splice;
2. `q_0` and `sigma` are constant along each chain;
3. across the splice,
   \[
   (q_0,\sigma)\mapsto
   \begin{cases}
   (q_0+1,\sigma), & q_0<m-1,\\
   (0,\sigma+1), & q_0=m-1;
   \end{cases}
   \]
4. current `epsilon4` obeys the `072/073` formulas
   \[
   a=1_{\{q_0=m-1\}},
   \qquad
   b=1_{\{q_0+\sigma\equiv1\}} \vee
     (1_{\{q_0=m-1\}}\wedge 1_{\{\sigma\neq1\}}).
   \]

Then the map
\[
\pi(x) := (\beta(x), \delta(x)),
\qquad
\delta(x):= q_0(x)+m\sigma(x)\pmod{m^2},
\]
is a deterministic global quotient on the accessible regular chain union, and
current `epsilon4` factors exactly through `pi`.

#### Proof

Inside a chain, `beta` decreases by `1` and `delta` is fixed because both
digits `(q_0,\sigma)` are fixed there.
At the splice, `beta` resets from `0` to `m-1`, while the digit update in
assumption 3 is exactly `delta -> delta+1 (mod m^2)`.
So the successor on `(\beta,\delta)` is deterministic.

Assumption 4 makes `(a,b)` a readout of `delta`.
Substituting those bits into the `073` decorated current-event rule gives the
displayed formula for `\bar\epsilon_4(\beta,\delta)`.
Hence current `epsilon4` factors exactly through `(\beta,\delta)`. ∎

### Corollary 2.2

The static decorated object `(beta,a,b)` is a quotient of
`Q_m^{dyn}`, but not the final global object.
It is exact **on each chain** and as a current-event readout, but it forgets the
splice state `delta`.

### Relation to the saved `072` candidate

The saved candidate called `beta_plus_q_plus_sigma` in
`state_chain_union_quotients.json` is exactly this bridge:
it is the state `(\beta,q_0,\sigma)`, equivalently `(\beta,\delta)`.

## 3. Precise obstruction and minimality

The real obstruction is not “no bridge exists.”
The real obstruction is sharper:

> any exact global bridge must remember the full boundary odometer state
> `delta`; the static type `(a,b)` is too compressed because it does not
> determine the next-chain future.

I now make that precise.

### 3.1 Boundary right-congruence is trivial

For a boundary state `delta=(q_0,\sigma)`, let
\[
\lambda(\delta) := (a(\delta), b(\delta)).
\]
Let `T(delta)=delta+1` be the chain-step/splice map.
Consider the infinite future type word
\[
\Lambda(\delta) := \bigl(\lambda(T^n\delta)\bigr)_{n\ge0}.
\]

### Proposition 3.1

The map `delta -> Lambda(delta)` is injective on `Z/m^2`.

#### Proof

Write `delta=(q_0,\sigma)`.

First recover `q_0`.
The first coordinate `a(T^n\delta)` equals `1` exactly when the low digit is
`m-1`.
Under `T`, the low digit increments by `1` each chain.
So the first index `r` with `a(T^r\delta)=1` is
\[
r = m-1-q_0.
\]
Hence `q_0 = m-1-r`.

Now recover `sigma`.

At the same index `r`, the low digit is already `m-1`, so
\[
b(T^r\delta)=1 \iff \sigma \neq 1.
\]
Thus if `b(T^r\delta)=0`, then `sigma=1`.

Assume now `b(T^r\delta)=1`, so `sigma\neq1`.
After one more chain step the odometer wraps the low digit and increments the
high digit:
\[
T^{r+1}\delta = (0,\sigma+1).
\]
For the next `m-1` chain steps the high digit stays `sigma+1` while the low
digit runs through `0,1,\dots,m-2`.
At offset `t` in that interval the state is `(t,\sigma+1)`, so
\[
b(T^{r+1+t}\delta)=1
\iff t+(\sigma+1) \equiv 1 \pmod m.
\]
Hence there is a unique index `t \in \{0,\dots,m-2\}` with
\[
b(T^{r+1+t}\delta)=1.
\]
That index is
\[
t=
\begin{cases}
0, & \sigma=0,\\
m-\sigma, & \sigma\in\{2,\dots,m-1\}.
\end{cases}
\]
So `sigma` is recovered from the offset of the first post-wrap `b=1` event.

Therefore both digits `(q_0,\sigma)` are determined by the future type word.
So `delta -> Lambda(delta)` is injective. ∎

### Corollary 3.2 (minimal dynamic decoration)

Any deterministic quotient exact for current `epsilon4` on the global bridge
must separate distinct boundary states `delta`.

Equivalently, the boundary right-congruence is trivial.
So there is **no** smaller splice-compatible boundary memory than `delta`.

### 3.2 Full-state minimality

Now put `beta` back in.

### Proposition 3.3

Let
\[
x=(\beta,\delta)\in Q_m^{\mathrm{dyn}}.
\]
Then the future current-event word
\[
W_x := \bigl(\bar\epsilon_4(F^n x)\bigr)_{n\ge0}
\]
determines `x` uniquely.

#### Proof

The first time the word hits `wrap` is exactly `beta`.
So `beta` is recoverable from `W_x`.

After `beta+1` steps the system is at the carry-jump start of the next chain,
namely at
\[
F^{\beta+1}x = (m-1, T\delta).
\]
From that point on, the future current-event word decomposes canonically into
chain blocks because `carry_jump` marks the start of each block and `wrap`
marks the end.
Reading the two early post-carry sites in each block recovers the future type
word `\Lambda(T\delta)`.
By Proposition 3.1 this determines `T\delta`, and since `T` is bijective, it
determines `delta`.

Hence `W_x` determines both `beta` and `delta`, so it determines `x`. ∎

### Corollary 3.4 (smallest exact bridge)

On any splice-invariant accessible subset `A` of boundary states,
the smallest deterministic quotient exact for current `epsilon4` has exactly
\[
m\,|A|
\]
states, namely the bridge `(\beta,\delta)` with `\delta\in A`.

If the accessible boundary set is all of `Z/m^2`, then the smallest exact
bridge has exactly
\[
m^3
\]
states.

So the precise Researcher-1 obstruction is:

> the full boundary odometer state at splice is forced;
> the chain-static type `(a,b)` is not enough, and in fact no smaller
> splice-compatible dynamic decoration exists.

### 3.3 Why the static `(a,b)` bridge fails

This framework also rederives the `073` splice obstruction cleanly.
Many distinct `delta` share the same current type `(a,b)` but have different
next-chain futures, so successor is not a function of `(a,b)`.

On the full odometer the induced type-successor sets are

- `00 -> {00,01,10,11}`
- `01 -> {00,11}`
- `10 -> {00}`
- `11 -> {00,01}`

which is exactly the nondeterministic splice pattern seen in the `073` support.

## 5. What is proved, what is only checked, and what remains open

### Proved / argued here

- The correct splice-compatible decorated bridge is the boundary odometer
  `(\beta,\delta)`, equivalently `(\beta,q_0,\sigma)`.
- The static bridge `(beta,a,b)` is a quotient/readout of this object, not the
  final global state.
- Given the `072/073` formulas and splice law, current `epsilon4` factors
  exactly through `(\beta,\delta)`.
- The boundary right-congruence is trivial, so no smaller splice-compatible
  boundary memory exists.
- Therefore the smallest exact bridge on any splice-invariant accessible subset
  `A` has exactly `m |A|` states.

### Checked on saved data

- The formulas for `(a,b)` from `(q_0,\sigma)` hold with zero failures on every
  extracted regular chain in the frozen `047` dataset for `m=5,7,9,11`.
- The odometer splice law holds with zero failures on every observed regular
  chain transition in the same range.
- The full boundary state set is realized on the saved range `m=7,9,11`.
- The exact candidate `beta_plus_q_plus_sigma` from the saved `072` file has
  precisely the bridge sizes predicted by this odometer picture.

### Still open after this note

- I have **not** proved from theorem-side structure that the full boundary set
  `A = Z/m^2` is accessible for every odd `m`; I only checked it on the saved
  range `m=7,9,11`.
- I have **not** proved that this bridge already descends to the intended
  admissible/local feature class.
- I have **not** extended the fresh direct check past the frozen saved range.
