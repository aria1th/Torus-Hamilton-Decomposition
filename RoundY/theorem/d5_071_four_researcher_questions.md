# D5 Four Researcher Questions 071

This note turns the current `071` bridge picture into four distinct questions
for collaborators.

## Researcher 1: Bridge existence

Question:

> Do the per-chain exact marked-chain quotients glue into one exact global
> quotient on the accessible union of chains?

What to aim for:

- prove existence of the exact bridge
- or prove a contradiction to such a bridge
- keep the statement on the exact quotient itself, not on broad local-rule
  families

Success criterion:

- a theorem or precise obstruction about globalization of the per-chain exact
  quotients

## Researcher 2: Symmetry versus asymmetry

Question:

> Is the chain offset globally removable, so carry is rotatable, or is carry
> essentially asymmetric across chains?

What to aim for:

- find a global normalization that removes the offset
- or prove that no exact admissible normalization can do so

Success criterion:

- a clear theorem or counterexample-style obstruction distinguishing the two
  pictures

## Researcher 3: Realization / clock descent

Question:

> Assuming the right exact global quotient exists, is it exact enough for
> current `epsilon4` that the canonical clock descends by corner-time?

What to aim for:

- show that the short corner is visible on the quotient
- derive corner-time descent
- identify the precise exactness hypothesis that is sufficient

Success criterion:

- a realization theorem for the canonical clock on the exact quotient

## Researcher 4: Compute support

Question:

> What exact reduction object and quotient are actually supported by the data,
> beyond the already settled per-chain picture?

What to aim for:

- validate the marked-chain rule on larger moduli
- validate candidate union-of-chains quotients
- test offset-normalization candidates
- test exactness of current `epsilon4` and the short-corner detector there

What not to do:

- no broad search
- no generic controller scans
- no reopening dead small-family branches

Success criterion:

- a sharper exact reduction object with saved data and logs, or a concrete
  contradiction to a candidate bridge
