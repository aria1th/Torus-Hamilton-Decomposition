## 1. Source of the repair

The gate note
[d5_278_routee_refined_family_large_odd_gate.md](/data/angel/repos/modulation-guidance/Torus-Hamilton-Decomposition/RoundY/theorem/d5_278_routee_refined_family_large_odd_gate.md)
showed that the best `277` refined-family rule fails exactness from `m=11`
onward.

The key structural discovery is that the recurring bad targets are generated by
one single source family inside the refined class

- `S2({1,2,3,4})|G0|M0`,

namely

- `u_m = (m-1, 6, m-1, m-1, m-1)`.

The default refined-family permutation on that source contributes the repeated
incoming colors in all three `278` bad targets.

## 2. The repair

Keep the best `277` refined family unchanged everywhere except on that single
source family `u_m`.

At `u_m`, replace the local color-`0,1,2` permutation by

- `(1,2,0)`.

So this is not a new search family. It is a one-point class-guarded repair on
top of the best `277` rule.

## 3. Checked exactness

The repaired family is exact on the checked odd range

- `m = 7,9,11,13,15,17`.

This is the main reason the repair matters. It does not merely kill the first
`278` witness and create another one nearby. On the checked range, exactness
survives.

## 4. Checked color-2 Hamilton closure

Under the repaired family, color `2` is Hamilton on the checked odd range

- `m = 7,9,11,13,15,17`.

So the `275–276` residual color-`2` problem is no longer open on the checked
range once this one-point repair is inserted.

## 5. What remains open

This repair does **not** finish the full five-color assembly theorem.

On the checked range, colors `0` and `1` still have non-Hamilton cycle
profiles. So the right reading of `279` is:

- the first refined-family line was already close;
- the `278` large-odd gate really was caused by one tiny missing local choice;
- after fixing that choice, color `2` drops out of the live frontier.

So the remaining graph-side burden is now narrower again:

- exact repaired family in hand,
- color `2`, `3`, `4` closed on the checked range,
- residual theorem work concentrated on colors `0` and `1`.

## 6. Bottom line

The `274–278` line was not a dead end.

It was one local decision away from a materially better family.

`279` shows that:

- the `278` witness family is repaired by one class-guarded source correction,
- the repaired family remains exact on the checked odd range,
- and color `2` becomes Hamilton throughout that checked range.

So the next honest target is no longer “repair color `2` somehow.”

It is: understand the residual color-`0/1` behavior in this repaired family.
