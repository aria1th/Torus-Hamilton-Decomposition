The proof picture is much tighter now.

The formal part is clean:
[
\tau(Fx)=\tau(x)-1 \qquad (\tau(x)>0).
]
So the whole uniform theorem reduces to the boundary (\tau=0).

On the carry_jump branch, I found an explicit checked formula on (m=5,7,9,11):
[
R_{\mathrm{cj}}(s,v,\lambda)=
\begin{cases}
0,& s+v+\lambda\equiv 2 \pmod m,\
1,& s=1 \text{ and } s+v+\lambda\not\equiv 2 \pmod m,\
m-2,& \text{otherwise.}
\end{cases}
]
Equivalently, on the checked range,
[
q \equiv 1-s-v-\lambda \pmod m
]
on the carry_jump boundary. That packages the `053` zero-reset fiber into a much stronger current-state candidate.

On the `other` branch, the checked data split it into two concrete grouped-delta subtypes:
[
(0,0,1,0)\quad\text{and}\quad(1,0,0,0).
]
Their reset values are strikingly clean on the checked range:
[
(0,0,1,0)\mapsto m-4,
]
and
[
(1,0,0,0)\mapsto
\begin{cases}
m-3,& s=1,\
0,& s\neq 1.
\end{cases}
]
So the real issue on the `other` branch is just proving that the subtype is a function of ((s,u,\lambda)) uniformly in odd (m).

That gives the exact proof reduction:

* `wrap` should be immediate,
* `carry_jump` reduces to one branch lemma `CJ`,
* `other` reduces to one branch lemma `OTH`.

So I did not fully prove the uniform odd-(m) boundary reset theorem yet. What I did get is the right sharper target:
