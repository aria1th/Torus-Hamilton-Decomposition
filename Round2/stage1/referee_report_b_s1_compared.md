I compared the current PDF  against the earlier uploaded LaTeX draft. As a referee, my view is that this revision **materially improves the paper’s flow and explanatory power**, and **modestly improves its mathematical beauty**, but it does **not** transform the even case into a genuinely clean or intuitive argument.

The strongest improvement is that the revised version now gives the even case a real narrative arc. The abstract and introduction no longer describe Route E merely as an explicit construction with closed-form return maps; they now present a clear obstruction/repair story: a primary low-layer geometry already closes colors 0 and 2, color 1 fails for (m\equiv 4 \pmod 6) by falling into three residue-3 strands, boundary-only corrections do not fix that failure, and an extra affine defect family is therefore forced before a finite splice closes the cycles. In the earlier draft I inspected directly, this necessity story was much less explicit; the even case read more like a constructed certificate.

This is reinforced by the reorganization of Section 4. The revised paper explicitly says the argument should be read in four stages and inserts a subsection on “primary geometry, obstruction, and forced repair.” Proposition 4 identifies the first genuine obstruction in the primary geometry, and Proposition 5 shows that deleting only the added Case II affine family makes the induced lane maps noninjective, so the extra family is not cosmetic but necessary bulk repair. That is the single biggest elevation in flow: the proof now explains *why this repair* rather than only *what repair*.

The design paragraphs and closing remarks are also better. The Route E definition now says that layer 0 carries both the baseline defect geometry and the final repair, and explains that the added affine family changes the interior height ordering for colors 1 and 0, breaking the residue-3 obstruction and leaving only a finite splice problem. Later remarks sharpen this further: the paper now says the Case II lines do more than add endpoint corrections, turning the primary color-1 drift into the repaired pattern and recasting the color-0 (m \bmod 12) split as a cyclic reordering inside a residue-4 splice picture. Theorem 5 itself is now written as a deductive chain, not just an appendix pointer, and Remark 6 explicitly frames the final step as a finite-defect normal form with arithmetic family-blocks and splice permutations.

So my comparative judgment is this: the revision has moved the paper from “technically correct but aesthetically opaque” to “technically correct with a coherent conceptual spine.” That is a real improvement. But the gain in **beauty** is only partial. The paper itself now openly acknowledges that what is still missing is a clean Route E-specific “critical-lane theorem” that would read off the repaired arithmetic family-blocks and splice permutation directly from the affine defect heights, and a more uniform explanation of the color-0 split. I agree with that self-diagnosis. The revised draft makes the ad hoc structure intelligible; it does not remove the ad hoc structure.

As a referee, I would therefore change my editorial stance. If the earlier draft would have merited **major revision on exposition/presentation grounds**, this version reduces that to **minor revision**. I would no longer insist on a major revision simply because the proof is not intrinsically beautiful. The present draft is much more readable and much easier to defend as publishable mathematics. My remaining requests would be minor: tighten the still-informal “absorbing bridge vertices into adjacent runs” language in the splice proposition, and smooth a few residual patches where the argument still sounds assembled rather than proved from a single clean principle.

**Decision on the revised version:** **Minor revision**; in some journals, **accept** would also be defensible.
